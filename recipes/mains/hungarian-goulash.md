# Hungarian Goulash

**Cuisine:** Hungarian | **Freezer-friendly:** Yes | **Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 90 min

## Shared Stew Base
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 large onions, diced
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 tbsp sweet Hungarian paprika
- 1 tsp ground caraway seeds
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 3 bay leaves
- 1 tsp brown sugar
- 1/2 cup tomato sauce
- 2 tbsp red wine vinegar (or balsamic vinegar)
- 4 cups broth (see versions below)
- 1 lb baby potatoes, halved (or Yukon Gold, cubed)
- 2 large carrots, thick coins
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 1 tsp salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste

## Garnish
- Fresh parsley, chopped
- Crusty bread or egg noodles for serving

---

## Vegetarian Version
**Protein:** 2 cans (15 oz each) kidney beans, drained and rinsed + 1 block (14 oz) extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed

*Use vegetable broth for this version.*

1. Press tofu 15 min. Cut into 3/4-inch cubes.
2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Pan-fry tofu until golden on all sides, ~6 min. Remove and set aside.
3. In the same pot, add remaining 1 tbsp olive oil. Sauté onions until soft and golden, ~6 min. Add garlic, stir 1 min.
4. Add paprika, caraway, oregano, bay leaves, and brown sugar. Stir 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Stir in tomato sauce, red wine vinegar, and vegetable broth. Add potatoes, carrots, and bell pepper. Season with salt and pepper.
6. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cover and cook 25 min until potatoes are fork-tender.
7. Add kidney beans and tofu. Simmer uncovered 10 min to thicken the stew.
8. Remove bay leaves. Adjust salt, pepper, and vinegar to taste.
9. Serve over egg noodles or with crusty bread. Garnish with parsley.

---

## Meat Version (No Soy / No Dairy)
**Protein:** 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into 1-inch pieces

*Use beef broth (no salt added) for this version.*

1. Pat beef dry. Season generously with salt and pepper.
2. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large Dutch oven over high heat. Sear beef in batches until browned on all sides, ~5 min per batch. Remove and set aside.
3. Reduce heat to medium. Sauté onions in the same pot until soft and golden, ~6 min. Add garlic, stir 1 min.
4. Add paprika, caraway, oregano, bay leaves, and brown sugar. Stir 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Stir in tomato sauce, red wine vinegar, and beef broth. Return beef to the pot with any juices. Add carrots and bell pepper.
6. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cover and cook 60 min, stirring occasionally.
7. Add potatoes. Cover and simmer 25 more min until potatoes and beef are fork-tender. Add water or broth if liquid reduces too much.
8. Remove bay leaves. Adjust salt, pepper, and vinegar to taste.
9. Serve over egg noodles or with crusty bread. Garnish with parsley.

## Meal Prep Notes
- Freezes well for up to 3 months. Flavor improves the next day.
- Store noodles/bread separately from stew.
- Keeps 4–5 days in the fridge. Stew thickens as it sits — add a splash of broth when reheating.
- Potatoes may soften further on reheating — this is fine, they thicken the sauce.
