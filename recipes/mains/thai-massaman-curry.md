# Thai Massaman Curry

**Cuisine:** Thai | **Freezer-friendly:** Yes | **Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 35 min

## Shared Curry Base
- 2 tbsp massaman curry paste (store-bought, or sub red curry paste + massaman spices below)
- 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
- 1 cup vegetable broth
- 1 lb baby potatoes, halved (or Yukon Gold, cubed)
- 1 large carrot, roll-cut or thick coins
- 1 small onion, quartered
- 2 tbsp peanut butter (or cashew butter)
- 1 tbsp coconut aminos (or soy sauce for veg version)
- 1 tbsp brown sugar or coconut sugar
- 1 tbsp tamarind paste (or 1 tbsp lime juice)
- 1 tsp salt, to taste

## Massaman Spice Boost (optional, if using plain red curry paste)
- 1/2 tsp ground coriander
- 1/2 tsp ground cumin
- 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1/4 tsp ground cardamom
- Pinch of ground cloves
- Pinch of nutmeg

## Garnish
- 1/4 cup roasted peanuts or cashews
- Fresh Thai basil or cilantro
- Lime wedges
- Steamed jasmine rice for serving

---

## Vegetarian Version
**Protein:** 1 block (14 oz) extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed + 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained

1. Press tofu 15 min. Cut into 1-inch cubes.
2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Pan-fry tofu until golden on all sides, ~6 min. Remove and set aside.
3. In the same pot, add curry paste (and massaman spice boost if using). Stir-fry 1 min until fragrant.
4. Add the thick cream from the top of the coconut milk. Stir and cook 2 min.
5. Add remaining coconut milk, broth, potatoes, carrots, and onion. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cook 15 min until potatoes are fork-tender.
6. Stir in peanut butter, coconut aminos (or soy sauce), brown sugar, tamarind paste, and chickpeas. Simmer 5 min.
7. Add tofu back in. Stir gently. Adjust salt and sweetness.
8. Serve over jasmine rice. Top with peanuts, herbs, and a squeeze of lime.

---

## Meat Version (No Soy / No Dairy)
**Protein:** 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces

1. Season chicken with salt and pepper.
2. Heat 1 tbsp coconut oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear chicken until browned on all sides, ~5 min. Remove and set aside.
3. In the same pot, add curry paste (and massaman spice boost if using). Stir-fry 1 min until fragrant.
4. Add the thick cream from the top of the coconut milk. Stir and cook 2 min.
5. Add remaining coconut milk, broth, potatoes, carrots, and onion. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cook 15 min until potatoes are fork-tender.
6. Stir in peanut butter, coconut aminos, brown sugar, and tamarind paste. Simmer 5 min.
7. Return chicken to the pot. Simmer 5 more min until chicken is cooked through (165°F).
8. Adjust salt and sweetness. Serve over jasmine rice with peanuts, herbs, and lime.

## Meal Prep Notes
- Freezes well for up to 3 months. Store rice separately.
- Potatoes may soften further on reheating -- this is fine, they thicken the sauce.
- Keeps 4-5 days in the fridge.
