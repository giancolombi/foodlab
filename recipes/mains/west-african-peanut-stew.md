# West African Peanut Stew (Maafe)

**Cuisine:** West African (Senegalese) | **Freezer-friendly:** Yes (up to 3 months) | **Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 40 min

## Shared Base
- 2 tbsp olive oil (or coconut oil)
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 3 tbsp tomato paste
- 1/2 cup natural peanut butter (creamy)
- 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
- 3 cups broth (vegetable or chicken)
- 1 large sweet potato, cubed
- 2 cups chopped kale or collard greens
- 1 bell pepper, diced
- 1 carrot, diced
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/4 tsp cayenne (adjust to taste)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Juice of 1 lime

## Serve With
- White rice or couscous
- Chopped roasted peanuts + cilantro for garnish

---

## Vegetarian Version
**Protein:** 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained + 1/2 cup roasted peanuts

1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté onion until soft, ~5 min.
2. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 min.
3. Stir in tomato paste, cumin, paprika, cayenne. Cook 2 min.
4. Add peanut butter, stir until melted into the paste.
5. Pour in diced tomatoes and broth. Stir until smooth.
6. Add sweet potato, carrot, and bell pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
7. Cover and cook 25 min until sweet potato is tender.
8. Add kale and chickpeas. Cook 5 more min until kale wilts.
9. Squeeze in lime juice. Adjust salt.
10. Serve over rice, topped with crushed peanuts and cilantro.

---

## Meat Version (No Soy / No Dairy)
**Protein:** 1.5 lbs bone-in chicken thighs, skin removed

1. Season chicken with salt, cumin, and paprika.
2. Heat oil in a Dutch oven over medium-high. Brown chicken on both sides, ~4 min per side. Remove and set aside.
3. In the same pot, sauté onion until soft, ~5 min.
4. Add garlic, ginger, tomato paste, spices. Cook 2 min.
5. Stir in peanut butter until melted. Add tomatoes and chicken broth.
6. Add sweet potato, carrot, bell pepper. Return chicken to pot.
7. Bring to a boil, reduce to simmer. Cover and cook 30 min until chicken is cooked through.
8. Remove chicken, shred with two forks, return to pot.
9. Add kale, cook 5 min. Squeeze in lime juice.
10. Serve over rice, topped with crushed peanuts and cilantro.
