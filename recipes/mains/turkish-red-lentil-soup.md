# Turkish Red Lentil Soup (Mercimek Çorbası)

**Cuisine:** Turkish | **Freezer-friendly:** Yes (up to 3 months) | **Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 30 min

## Shared Base
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 large carrot, diced
- 1 medium potato, peeled and cubed
- 1.5 cups red lentils, rinsed
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 5 cups broth (vegetable or chicken)
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp turmeric
- 1/4 tsp cayenne (adjust to taste)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Juice of 1 lemon

## Serve With
- Lemon wedges
- Crusty bread
- Pinch of Aleppo pepper or paprika drizzled with olive oil on top

---

## Vegetarian Version
**Protein:** 1 can (15 oz) white beans (cannellini), drained + 2 tbsp butter stirred in at the end

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté onion until soft, ~5 min.
2. Add garlic, carrot, and potato. Cook 3 min, stirring occasionally.
3. Stir in tomato paste, cumin, paprika, turmeric, and cayenne. Cook 1 min.
4. Add red lentils and vegetable broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
5. Cover and cook 20 min until lentils and potato are completely soft.
6. Blend with an immersion blender until smooth and velvety.
7. Stir in white beans and butter. Simmer 5 min until heated through.
8. Squeeze in lemon juice. Adjust salt.
9. Serve with lemon wedges, a drizzle of olive oil, and a pinch of paprika on top.

---

## Meat Version (No Soy / No Dairy)
**Protein:** 1 lb ground chicken or ground beef

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Brown the ground meat, breaking it up, ~5 min. Remove and set aside.
2. In the same pot, sauté onion until soft, ~5 min.
3. Add garlic, carrot, and potato. Cook 3 min.
4. Stir in tomato paste, cumin, paprika, turmeric, and cayenne. Cook 1 min.
5. Add red lentils and chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
6. Cover and cook 20 min until lentils and potato are completely soft.
7. Remove about half the soup and blend it (or use an immersion blender lightly) to get a semi-chunky texture.
8. Return browned meat to the pot. Simmer 5 min.
9. Squeeze in lemon juice. Adjust salt.
10. Serve with lemon wedges, a drizzle of olive oil, and a pinch of paprika on top.
