# Peruvian Ají de Gallina

**Cuisine:** Peruvian | **Freezer-friendly:** Yes | **Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 40 min

## Shared Sauce Base
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2–3 tbsp ají amarillo paste (found in Latin grocery stores or online)
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1/2 tsp turmeric
- 1/2 cup walnuts, finely ground (this makes the sauce creamy — no dairy needed)
- 2 slices white bread, torn into pieces (thickener)
- 1.5 cups broth (chicken or vegetable)
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Serve With
- White rice
- Boiled yellow potatoes, halved
- Black olives
- Hard-boiled egg, halved

---

## Vegetarian Version
**Protein:** 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained + 1 can (15 oz) cannellini beans

1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté onion until soft, ~5 min.
2. Add garlic, ají amarillo paste, cumin, turmeric. Cook 2 min until fragrant.
3. Add ground walnuts and bread pieces. Stir 1 min.
4. Pour in vegetable broth. Stir and simmer 10 min until bread dissolves and sauce thickens.
5. Blend sauce with an immersion blender until smooth (or transfer to blender).
6. Return to pot. Add chickpeas and cannellini beans.
7. Simmer 10 min until heated through and sauce coats the beans.
8. Serve over rice with boiled potatoes, olives, and hard-boiled egg.

---

## Meat Version (No Soy / No Dairy)
**Protein:** 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs

1. Poach chicken thighs in salted water for 20 min until cooked through. Remove, shred with two forks. Reserve 1.5 cups poaching liquid as broth.
2. Heat oil in a large pot. Sauté onion until soft, ~5 min.
3. Add garlic, ají amarillo paste, cumin, turmeric. Cook 2 min.
4. Add ground walnuts and bread. Stir 1 min.
5. Pour in reserved chicken broth. Simmer 10 min, stirring until sauce thickens.
6. Blend until smooth with immersion blender.
7. Add shredded chicken. Stir to coat, simmer 5 min.
8. Serve over rice with boiled potatoes, olives, and hard-boiled egg.
