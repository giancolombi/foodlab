# Cuban Ropa Vieja

**Cuisine:** Cuban | **Freezer-friendly:** Yes | **Prep:** 15 min | **Cook:** 2 hrs (meat) / 45 min (veg)

## Shared Base
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large onion, sliced
- 2 bell peppers (red + yellow/orange), sliced
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp oregano
- 1/4 tsp allspice
- 1/2 cup white wine (or broth)
- 1 can (28 oz) whole peeled tomatoes, crushed by hand
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 carrot, halved (removed at end)
- 2 ribs celery, halved (removed at end)
- 1/3 cup pimento-stuffed green olives
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Serve With
- White rice
- Black beans (canned or cooked, seasoned with cumin and garlic)
- Fresh cilantro

---

## Vegetarian Version
**Protein:** 2 cans (14 oz each) young jackfruit, drained and shredded + 1 can (15 oz) black beans

1. Heat olive oil in a Dutch oven over medium heat. Sauté onion and peppers until soft, ~7 min.
2. Add garlic, tomato paste, cumin, oregano, allspice. Cook 2 min.
3. Add wine, scrape up fond. Add tomatoes, bay leaves, carrot, celery.
4. Stir in shredded jackfruit. Bring to a simmer.
5. Cover and cook on low 30–40 min until jackfruit is very tender and shreds apart easily.
6. Remove carrot, celery, and bay leaves.
7. Stir in olives and black beans. Cook 5 more min.
8. Serve over rice with cilantro.

---

## Meat Version (No Soy / No Dairy)
**Protein:** 2 lbs flank steak

1. Season flank steak with salt, pepper, cumin, and oregano.
2. Heat olive oil in a Dutch oven over high heat. Sear steak on both sides, ~3 min per side. Remove.
3. In the same pot, sauté onion and peppers until soft, ~7 min.
4. Add garlic, tomato paste, remaining cumin, oregano, allspice. Cook 2 min.
5. Add wine, scrape up fond. Add tomatoes, bay leaves, carrot, celery.
6. Return steak to pot, submerge in sauce.
7. Cover, bake at 350°F for 1.5–2 hours until steak shreds easily with two forks.
8. Remove steak, shred into long strands, return to pot.
9. Remove carrot, celery, bay leaves. Stir in olives.
10. Serve over rice with black beans and cilantro.
