# Spanish Chickpea & Spinach Stew

**Cuisine:** Spanish | **Freezer-friendly:** Yes | **Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 25 min

## Shared Stew Base
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 20 blanched almonds
- 2 tsp sweet smoked paprika (pimentón)
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1/2 cup tomato sauce
- 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 3 cups vegetable broth
- Pinch of saffron threads (optional — or 1/2 tsp turmeric)
- 10 oz fresh spinach (or 1 bag frozen spinach)
- 1 tbsp sherry vinegar (or red wine vinegar)
- 1 tsp salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste

## Almond-Herb Paste (Picada)
- Reserved toasted almonds (from above)
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 clove garlic
- Pinch of salt

## Serving Suggestions
- Crusty bread for dipping
- Squeeze of lemon juice
- Extra smoked paprika drizzle with olive oil

---

## Vegetarian Version
**Protein:** 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas (already in base) + 1 block (14 oz) extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed

1. Press tofu 15 min. Cut into 3/4-inch cubes.
2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Pan-fry tofu until golden on all sides, ~6 min. Remove and set aside.
3. In the same pot, add remaining 1 tbsp olive oil. Toast almonds and 1 clove garlic until lightly golden, ~3 min. Transfer to a mortar or small food processor with parsley and a pinch of salt. Pound or pulse into a rough paste. Set aside.
4. Sauté diced onion in the same pot until translucent, ~4 min. Add minced garlic, smoked paprika, and cumin. Stir 30 seconds.
5. Add sherry vinegar and tomato sauce. Simmer 2 min until thickened.
6. Add chickpeas, broth, and saffron (or turmeric). Season with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cover and cook 10 min.
7. Stir in the almond paste and spinach. Cook 2–3 min until spinach wilts.
8. Add tofu back in. Stir gently. Adjust salt and vinegar to taste.
9. Serve in bowls with crusty bread.

---

## Meat Version (No Soy / No Dairy)
**Protein:** 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces

1. Season chicken with salt, pepper, and 1/2 tsp smoked paprika.
2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear chicken until browned, ~5 min. Remove and set aside.
3. In the same pot, add remaining 1 tbsp olive oil. Toast almonds and 1 clove garlic until lightly golden, ~3 min. Transfer to a mortar or small food processor with parsley and a pinch of salt. Pound or pulse into a rough paste. Set aside.
4. Sauté diced onion in the same pot until translucent, ~4 min. Add minced garlic, smoked paprika, and cumin. Stir 30 seconds.
5. Add sherry vinegar and tomato sauce. Simmer 2 min until thickened.
6. Add chickpeas, broth, and saffron (or turmeric). Season with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. Cover and cook 10 min.
7. Stir in the almond paste and spinach. Cook 2–3 min until spinach wilts.
8. Return chicken to the pot. Simmer 5 more min until chicken is cooked through (165°F).
9. Adjust salt and vinegar to taste. Serve in bowls with crusty bread.

## Meal Prep Notes
- Freezes well for up to 3 months. The chickpeas hold up great on reheating.
- Keeps 4–5 days in the fridge. Stew thickens as it sits — add a splash of broth when reheating.
- Spinach may darken on freezing but flavor stays the same.
