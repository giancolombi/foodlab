# Lentil Stroganoff

**Cuisine:** Eastern European | **Freezer-friendly:** Yes | **Prep:** 10 min | **Cook:** 35 min

## Shared Base
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 large onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 8 oz mushrooms, sliced
- 1.5 cups green or brown lentils
- 3 cups vegetable broth
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp coconut aminos (or soy sauce for the vegetarian version)
- Salt, pepper, paprika to taste

**Veggies to hide:** Spinach, peas, carrots, zucchini — all blend right in.

---

## Vegetarian Version
**Protein:** 1.5 cups green or brown lentils (in base)
**Finish:** 1/2 cup Greek yogurt or sour cream (stir in at the end, off heat)

1. Heat oil, sauté onion and mushrooms until golden, ~8 min.
2. Add garlic, cook 1 min.
3. Add lentils, broth, mustard, coconut aminos (or soy sauce), paprika.
4. Bring to a boil, reduce to simmer. Cover 25 min until lentils are tender.
5. Remove from heat. Stir in yogurt/sour cream.
6. Serve over egg noodles or black rice.

---

## Meat Version (No Soy / No Dairy)
**Protein:** Lentils (in base) — optionally swap half the lentils for 1/2 lb ground beef
**Finish:** 1/2 cup full-fat coconut milk or cashew cream (stir in at the end, off heat)

1. Heat oil. If using ground beef, brown it first, ~5 min. Sauté onion and mushrooms until golden, ~8 min.
2. Add garlic, cook 1 min.
3. Add lentils (3/4 cup if using beef), broth, mustard, coconut aminos, paprika.
4. Bring to a boil, reduce to simmer. Cover 25 min until lentils are tender.
5. Remove from heat. Stir in coconut milk or cashew cream.
6. Serve over egg noodles or black rice.

---
